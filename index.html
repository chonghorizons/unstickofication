The Method - described

The method is called 30-10-30-10-30. This is because it is:

  • 30 minutes of do-it!
  • 10 minutes of break/rest/reward/treat.
  • 30 minutes of do-it!
  • 10 minutes of break/rest/reward/treat.
  • 30 minutes of do-it!

It works because you break up the "work" and the "treat". Those people who say "the work is the reward"... well they can go climb Mt Everest and go skydiving while taking the LSAT. For the rest of us, work is drudgery, especially getting started.

So, 30-10-30-10-30 puts a light at the end of the tunnel. And the middle. And the front. You work for 30 (work, hard). Then 10 minutes of break/treat. Your brain won't be spooked because you'll see the break coming up. Knowing there is a break, the idea is you can put up with "work" for 30 minutes.

If 30 minutes sounds like too long, for now, just trust us. We'll adjust this up or down depending on where you are. 30 is a good start.
